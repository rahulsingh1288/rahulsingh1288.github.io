About me

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at MIT,LIDS, where I work with Prof. Eytan Modiano on designing control policies for stochastic networks serving real-time traffic. I obtained my Ph.D. from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University, College Station, Tx under the supervision of Prof. P. R. Kumar. During my thesis, I worked on developing decentralized algorithms for optimal operation of uncertain Cyberphysical Systems using online Machine Learning techniques.

My research interests include

  • Distributed Algorithms for Networks such as Overlay Networks, Content Delivery Networks, Cloud-based networking.

  • Secure Operation of Cyber-Physical Systems using Risk Sensitive decision making under uncertainity

  • Operation of Electricity Markets having unreliable (stochastic) renewable energy producers.

Research

Several of my research projects have centered around developing Online Machine Learning algorithms which provide

  • Decentralized control algorithms for multi-hop stochastic networks in which data packets have to meet end-to-end deadlines

  • Optimal stochastic bidding strategies in an electricity market

  • Stochastic pricing mechanisms in cloud based services that maximize the net revenue of the service provider


Additionally, I have also characterized the performance versus risk trade-off in stochastic networks. These include,

  • Mean-Variance trade-off in inter-delivery times of data packets

  • Risk sensitive decision making in stochastic networks
